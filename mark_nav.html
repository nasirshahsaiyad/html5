The Himalaya is the highest and youngest mountain range in the world, stretching across five countries: India, Nepal, Bhutan, China, and Pakistan. Formed millions of years ago by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, the Himalaya continues to rise slowly each year due to ongoing geological activity. This majestic range is home to some of the world's tallest peaks, including Mount Everest, which stands at 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet), making it the highest point on Earth. The Himalaya plays a crucial role in regulating the climate of the Indian subcontinent, acting as a barrier that blocks cold winds from Central Asia, which helps create the monsoon season. Numerous rivers, such as the Ganges, Yamuna, Indus, and Brahmaputra, originate from its glaciers, providing water to millions of people. The region is rich in biodiversity, with rare species such as the snow leopard, red panda, and Himalayan monal thriving in its forests and alpine meadows. Additionally, the Himalaya holds deep cultural and spiritual significance, being home to ancient monasteries, temples, and sacred pilgrimage sites like Kedarnath, Badrinath, and Mount Kailash. The range attracts adventurers and trekkers from around the world, offering famous trekking routes such as the Everest Base Camp, Annapurna Circuit, and Manaslu Trek. Overall, the Himalaya is not only a natural wonder but also a lifeline for the millions who depend on its resources for survival.
